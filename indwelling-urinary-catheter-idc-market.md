<p><p>The Indwelling Urinary Catheter (IDC) market is primarily divided into two main types: Indwelling Silicone Catheters and Indwelling Latex Catheters. Silicone catheters are biocompatible, reducing the risk of irritation and infection, and are often preferred for long-term use due to their durability. In contrast, latex catheters are typically more economical and flexible but may cause allergic reactions in some patients. Both types play crucial roles in managing urinary retention and facilitating patient comfort in clinical settings.</p></p>
